FAQ

Is it really free? What's the catch?

Really free. Tab Buddy stores your data in your own browser, and sync uses client-side encryption on infrastructure that costs pennies at any scale — so there's no cost that would force a subscription. If it saves your bacon, you can leave a tip on Ko-fi.

What happens if I forget my sync passphrase?

Nobody can recover it — not even us. That's the trade for genuine end-to-end encryption. Your local data is unaffected; you'd just set up a fresh sync with a new passphrase and your sessions re-upload. Store the passphrase in your password manager when you set it up.

Does it preserve Chrome's native tab groups?

Yes — names, colours, collapsed state, and order, on both save and restore. This is the gap that made us build Tab Buddy: the older session managers flatten your groups into a plain list.

I'm coming from OneTab / Session Buddy / Toby. Will my data survive?

Yes. Paste or drop your export on the Import page and it comes across — Toby lists even become named tab groups. There's an import step right in the welcome flow.

Why does the thumbnails option ask for a scary permission?

Capturing a preview of a page needs Chrome's "read all sites" permission — there is no narrower one. That's exactly why thumbnails are off by default and opt-in: everything else works without it, and the captures never leave your device either way.
