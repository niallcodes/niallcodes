What Nope does

Every category of web annoyance. One tool.

🍪

Rejects cookie banners

Where a site's consent platform is recognised, Nope genuinely declines non-essential cookies — real privacy, not just a hidden banner. Powered by the open-source DuckDuckGo AutoConsent rules.

🫧

Honest about it

When a banner can only be hidden (not rejected), Nope says so, per site: "Rejected via OneTrust" vs "Banner hidden". No other tool tells you the difference.

Newsletter modals, "sign in to continue" walls, chat bubbles, notification nags, "open in app" banners, survey popups — each with its own toggle.

Element picker

Cookies, honestly

Reject-first. Hide as fallback. Always transparent.

1 — Recognised → rejected

Known consent platforms (OneTrust, Cookiebot, Quantcast, Didomi and hundreds more) get their own "reject" flow driven for you. Fewer cookies actually set.

2 — Unrecognised → hidden

Unknown banners are hidden so you can read in peace — and Nope tells you the site may still set cookies, instead of pretending otherwise.

3 — You see what fired

The toolbar panel shows exactly what happened on every site, and everything is pausable and reversible per site.

FAQ

Fair questions.

Is it really free?

Completely. Every feature, for everyone, forever — no premium tier, no trial, no locked settings. Nope is supported by voluntary Ko-fi donations from people who like it. That's the whole business model.

Is Nope an ad blocker?

No. Nope removes annoyances and handles cookie consent — it does not block ads, and never will. That's a deliberate boundary: sites keep their revenue, you lose the junk.

Why does it need access to every website?

Cookie banners and popups appear on any site, so Nope's content script has to be able to run on any site to catch them. Everything happens locally in your browser: Nope makes no network requests, has no server, and collects nothing. The code that touches every page is exactly the code that can't phone home — see the privacy policy.

Does rejecting cookies break sites?

Rarely — Nope drives the site's own "reject" buttons, the same ones you'd click yourself. If a site ever misbehaves, pause Nope on that site with one toggle (or Alt+Shift+N) and it stays out of the way there.

What happens when Nope can't reject a banner?

It hides it and tells you it was only hidden — the site may still set cookies. Honesty over theatre. You can also report the site so the rules improve for everyone.

Does it circumvent paywalls?

No. Nope removes soft nags ("sign in to continue" overlays, newsletter walls) but doesn't unlock paid content or bypass hard paywalls.
